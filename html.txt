                    <h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
                        <span>🌱</span> The Bioscience Problem
                    </h2>
                    <p class="text-sm text-slate-600 leading-relaxed">
                        In Malaysian agro-product chains, minimizing the <strong>Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)</strong> is vital for profitability. Broiler chickens do not grow linearly; they follow a sigmoidal (S-shaped) logistic curve. 
                    </p>
                    <p class="text-sm text-slate-600 mt-2 leading-relaxed">
                        By identifying the exact point where the growth rate — the <strong>derivative f'(t)</strong> — peaks and begins to decelerate, farm managers and meat processors can determine the optimal harvest window before feed consumption outpaces muscle deposition.
                    </p>
                    <div class="mt-4 pt-3 border-t border-slate-100 text-xs text-slate-500">
                        <strong>Data Source Reference:</strong> Baseline parameters adapted from the <em>Department of Veterinary Services (DVS) Malaysia Broiler Industry Performance Guidelines (Cobb 500 Breed Standard)</em>.
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="bg-slate-900 text-slate-100 p-6 rounded-2xl shadow-md space-y-4">
                    <h2 class="text-xl font-bold text-emerald-400 flex items-center gap-2">
                        <span>📊</span> The Calculus Toolkit
                    </h2>
                    
                    <div>
                        <span class="block text-xs uppercase tracking-wider text-slate-400 font-semibold">Growth Function f(t)</span>
                        <div class="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg font-mono text-xs overflow-x-auto text-emerald-300 mt-1">
                            f(t) = L / (1 + a * e^(-b * t))
                        </div>
                        <p class="text-xs text-slate-400 mt-1">Predicts total biomass (grams) at day <strong>t</strong>.</p>
                    </div>

                    <div>
                        <span class="block text-xs uppercase tracking-wider text-slate-400 font-semibold">Derivative f'(t) (Rate of Change)</span>
                        <div class="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg font-mono text-xs overflow-x-auto text-amber-300 mt-1">
                            f'(t) = (L * a * b * e^(-b * t)) / (1 + a * e^(-b * t))^2
                        </div>
                        <p class="text-xs text-slate-400 mt-1">Calculates exact growth speed (grams/day) at day <strong>t</strong>.</p>
                    </div>
